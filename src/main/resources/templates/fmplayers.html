Jean-Pierre Papin has described football as a "universal language". Footballers across the world and at almost any level may regularly attract large crowds of spectators, and players are the focal points of widespread social phenomena such as association football culture.
In association football there are four traditional types of specialties (positions): goalkeepers (goalies), defenders (full-backs), midfielders (half-backs), forwards (attackers). Special purpose positions include such performers like sweepers, stoppers, second forwards (under-attackers), wingers, insiders, etc. Goalkeepers: good reflexes, communication with defence. Centre-backs: good heading and tackling ability, height. Full-backs: pace, stamina, anticipation, tackling and marking abilities. Central midfielders: stamina, passing ability, team responsibility. Wingers: pace, technical ability like dribbling and close control. Forwards: finishing ability, composure, technical ability, heading ability.
